The Moment

Shortly before discharge in July 2017, a direct protest occurred on the ward.

The concern raised was simple: the severe foot-centred symptoms that had triggered the referral and admission had not been explained or resolved.

The protest questioned how discharge could occur while the initiating clinical problem remained unanswered.

Witnessed Event

This exchange took place in the presence of clinical staff and medical students during a ward round.

It was not a retrospective reconstruction but a contemporaneous objection raised at the moment the admission was closing.

Record Omission

The protest does not appear in the discharge documentation that followed the admission.

As a result, the official record presents the discharge as a straightforward clinical progression rather than a contested closure.

Why This Matters

Events that occur at the point of diagnostic closure are particularly significant. They reveal what information and concerns were present at the moment final decisions were made.

When such events are absent from the record, later readers of the documentation may not see the full context in which those decisions occurred.
