The Case

In July 2017 a neurological admission resulted in a diagnosis of Parkinson’s disease being formally recorded in the patient's medical record. The admission involved neurological examination, imaging, and inpatient observation.

However, the sequence of events surrounding that admission raises a deeper question about how diagnostic conclusions become fixed within institutional systems.

The GP referral that initiated the admission described a concrete clinical problem: difficulty initiating movement and abnormal gait. The discharge summary that closed the admission recorded a diagnostic conclusion.

This archive exists to examine the sequence between those two moments.

Key Documents

Two documents sit at the centre of this case.

The first is the GP referral of 29 June 2017, which describes the clinical problem that triggered neurological investigation.

The second is the neurology discharge summary written at the conclusion of the July 2017 admission.

Together these two documents define the sequence examined throughout this archive: the clinical question posed at referral, and the diagnosis recorded at discharge.
