The Case

In July 2017 a neurological admission in Ireland resulted in a diagnosis of Parkinson’s disease being formally recorded in a patient's medical record. The admission involved examinations, clinical discussions, imaging, and observation. It concluded with discharge under a Parkinson’s treatment pathway.

However, the sequence of events surrounding that admission raises a deeper question about how diagnostic decisions become fixed within institutional systems.

The severe foot-centred symptoms that had originally triggered the referral remained unresolved at the moment the admission closed. Shortly before discharge, a direct objection was raised on the ward questioning how the admission could conclude while the initiating problem remained unexplained.

That objection does not appear in the discharge documentation that followed.

Once the diagnosis entered administrative systems — including statutory certification and long-term treatment pathways — it became the framework through which later clinical activity was interpreted.

Why This Archive Exists

Medicine operates through uncertainty. Diagnoses evolve as new information becomes available. For that reason, the central issue documented in this archive is not simply whether a diagnosis was correct or incorrect.

The issue is sequence.

When administrative recognition of a diagnosis occurs before the clinical inquiry that should test that diagnosis has been completed, the resulting record can become structurally difficult to revisit. Later decisions tend to follow the framework already recorded rather than reopening the original question.

This project reconstructs the sequence of events surrounding the 2017 admission and the institutional processes that followed in the years afterwards.

Diagnostic Sequence

The concept explored throughout this archive is simple but important.

In any investigative system — whether medical, scientific, or legal — inquiry must occur before conclusions are treated as final.

When that sequence is reversed, the resulting record may appear stable while the underlying inquiry remains incomplete. Over time the institutional systems surrounding that record begin to reinforce the conclusion that was recorded first.

Understanding how that sequence unfolded in this case is the purpose of the material presented here.
