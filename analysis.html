The Principle of Diagnostic Sequence

Every investigative system depends on sequence. Inquiry must occur before conclusions are treated as final. In medicine this usually means that diagnoses remain provisional until sufficient testing, observation, and clinical reasoning have been completed.

In most circumstances this process unfolds naturally. Clinicians test hypotheses, gather evidence, and gradually refine their understanding of the patient’s condition.

Difficulties arise when administrative or institutional processes attach finality to a working hypothesis before the inquiry that should test that hypothesis has been completed.

Administrative Finality

Modern healthcare systems contain many administrative mechanisms that rely on diagnostic labels. Insurance systems, statutory schemes, treatment pathways, and electronic records all require conditions to be recorded in a clear and stable form.

Once a diagnosis enters these systems it begins to function as an established fact. Prescriptions, referrals, clinical documentation, and future consultations tend to follow the framework already recorded.

This process is often practical and necessary. However, it can also create a form of institutional momentum that makes earlier diagnostic assumptions difficult to revisit.

Path Dependence in Clinical Systems

When a diagnostic label becomes embedded in records and administrative processes, later decisions tend to follow the path already established. Each new document or treatment decision references the earlier diagnosis rather than reopening the original question.

Over time this creates a form of path dependence. The system continues to operate consistently with the existing diagnosis even if new information begins to challenge it.

The Role of Sequence in This Case

The case documented in this project examines how diagnostic sequence unfolded during a neurological admission in 2017 and the years that followed.

The central question is not simply whether a diagnosis was correct or incorrect. Medicine frequently revises diagnoses as knowledge evolves.

The issue examined here concerns the order of events: how a diagnostic conclusion became administratively fixed while elements of the underlying clinical inquiry remained unresolved.

The documents presented in this archive allow the sequence of those events to be examined directly.

Purpose of This Archive

This project brings together narrative, timeline reconstruction, and primary records in order to make the sequence of events visible.

The intention is not to present a personal narrative alone, but to document how institutional processes interact with clinical reasoning in complex cases.

By presenting the materials in chronological and documentary form, readers can examine the events and reach their own conclusions about how diagnostic sequence operated in this case.
