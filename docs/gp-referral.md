Dear MAU/AMU,

Thank you for accepting review of this gentleman on Monday 3rd July as discussed by phone this afternoon.

He is aware to arrive for review at **8:30am**.

Mr Moloney transferred to our practice here in Briarhill in April where I noted him to have quite an unusual **festinant and stiff gait**. At that time he was attending with **bilateral foot issues** and attributed his gait difficulties to **callouses and corns**. His feet are not in a good state.

Over recent weeks it has become increasingly evident that he is having **trouble initiating movements at times**, and also has **marked stiffness and increased tone in both upper and lower limbs**, with **brisk reflexes**.

There may be **some cogwheeling at the wrist**, but there is **no overt resting tremor**.

He describes feeling that he **cannot relax his muscles**, and that this is most problematic when in bed, where he **claws his toes and cannot get comfortable**.

He had been doing reasonably well at work but has found it increasingly difficult performing tasks such as **gowning up and down when entering or leaving the clean room**.

More recently he reports that his **dexterity has worsened**, and he describes increasing difficulty with tasks such as **sorting papers into files** or carrying out **repetitive manual work**, for example **fitting scalpel blades to handles**, tasks which he would previously have been able to perform without difficulty.

He had initially attributed many of these symptoms to **anxiety**, and he does present with an **unusual affect**.

He is on **no regular medication** and **denies any illicit drug use**. He does report **previous alcohol use which may have been excessive**, having previously worked in a bar.

My concern is that there are **definite signs of increased tone with abnormal gait**, now **affecting his employment and daily activities**.

There may be a **Parkinsonian or other underlying neurological diagnosis**, but the condition **certainly appears to be deteriorating**.

I have referred him last month to **Neurology OPD**, but feel he needs **urgent assessment** as if things persist **he may lose his job**.
