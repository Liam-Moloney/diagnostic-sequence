## Key Extracts from the Discharge Summary and Clinic Note

The following passages are reproduced from the July 2017 discharge documentation and associated clinic correspondence. They are presented because they illuminate the clinical framing of the case and the sequence through which the diagnosis was recorded.

---

### Referral Context

> “39Y/O WITH DIFFICULTY INITIATING MOVEMENT”  
> “INCREASED TONE AND STIFFNESS IN ALL 4 LIMBS”  
> “COGWHEELING AT THE WRIST”  
> “ASSESS FOR NEURO OPINION AND ADVICE ON FURTHER INVESTIGATION”

---

### Symptom Narrative Recorded During Admission

> “I don’t synchronise walk in terms of the arms and legs so well.”

> “My writing has deteriorated to illegible.”

> “Balance is very bad — hasn’t fallen yet however he believes ‘a fall is imminent’.”

> “Dexterity is diminished.”

---

### Longstanding Foot History Recorded in the Same Document

> “As he looks back now he feels that the next issue was corns and calluses and the appearance of fallen arches in both feet.”

> “He has had to have treatment for these feet problem since 2009 and 2010 and they remain a problem.”

> “In 2012 he left Bar Working and for the most part this seemed to improve the pain in the feet.”

> “Therefore in 2012 he began to realise that the foot pain and the leg tension were not the same problem.”

---

### Neurological Examination

> “The gait is strikingly abnormal.”

> “He tends to walk almost with a footdrop appearance somewhat suggestive of a dystonic posture — NOT FOOT DROP.”

> “There is absolutely no arm swing.”

> “Gross bradykinesia.”

---

### Diagnostic Impression

> “A Young Onset Parkinsonism.”

> “? Young onset Parkinson’s disease.”

> “An ‘idiopathic’ synucleinopathy… Parkinson’s disease is suspected.”

> “However this may be another synucleinopathy or one of the other Parkinson’s plus disorders.”

---

### Conditional Separation of the Foot Problem

> “The leg symptoms including corns and callouses and burning may have been completely separate…”

> “However he has in his own mind markedly divorced the feelings of tension in the legs from the ‘corns and callouses’ issues and pains in the feet.”

---

### Medication Strategy

> “The greatest of care has been advised with consideration of adding Sinemet or Madopar.”

> “We were likely try and avoid these for as long as possible.”

> “It has been explained that ultimately sooner or later these types of treatments may be advised.”

---

### Podiatry Referral

> “Please refer Liam to outpatient Chiropody/Podiatry services.”

> “Podiatry referral has been sent to review issues with feet reported by the patient.”

---

### Opening of the Consultant Letter

> “Dear Aaron,  
> Liam was seen by our team and myself at Neurology Consults in AMU on Monday 3rd July.”

> “This is an unusual situation.”

> “We think that Liam unfortunately has a Parkinsonism.”

---

### Atypical Presentation

> “However in many respects it is of course an atypical presentation.”

> “Lack of prominent tremor.”

> “Very young age of onset.”

> “Symmetry is unusual.”

---

### Genetic Considerations

> “There is a question of whether the indicators are that he does have a PRKN gene disorder (PARK2).”

> “Aspects may point to rarer monogenic explanations such as PARK6 or PARK7.”

---

### Statement of Expertise

> “In reality, while I am not an expert on these conditions (I am not a movement disorders neurologist).”

> “However I suspect that whatever testing we might send may well be negative.”

---

### Levodopa Decision

> “Ordinarily we would use levodopa or dopamine agonists.”

> “However in these types of situations we tend to be extremely cautious in their usage.”

> “We will try to leave off on this for as long as possible.”

> “Indeed in time to come we may regret that we did not start this earlier.”

---

### DAT Scan Request

> “DAT scan please.”

> “In very young man age 39.”

> “Bilateral Parkinsonism.”

> “In many respects atypical.”

> “Walk is almost dystonically unusual.”

> “We suspect an autosomal recessive ‘idiopathic’ Parkinson’s.”

---

### DAT Scan Result

> “Marked truncation of radioisotope uptake by the basal ganglia bilaterally consistent with idiopathic Parkinson’s disease or Lewy body dementia.”

---

### Addendum After Imaging

> “It is abnormal as was expected.”

> “The report says Parkinson’s disease or Lewy body disease — however this is obviously not LBD.”
