Timeline

Key events from earliest symptoms to genetic confirmation.

2004
First persistent foot pain appears during bar work. Symptoms initially treated as local foot problems.
2009–2010
Repeated podiatry treatment for corns and calluses. Underlying cause remains unexplained.
2013
Symptoms briefly attributed to anxiety. Psychiatric evaluation finds no psychiatric disorder.
April 2017
New GP begins referral process due to worsening gait disturbance and chronic foot pain.
29 June 2017
Urgent GP referral to neurology describing abnormal gait and possible Parkinsonian features.
3 July 2017
Admission to University Hospital Galway for neurological assessment.
4 July 2017
Hospital case meeting discusses possible Parkinson’s diagnosis. Dopaminergic therapy not initiated.
5–9 July 2017
Hospital investigations including imaging, neurological examination, and therapy assessments.
9 July 2017
Long Term Illness certification completed for Parkinson’s disease.
10 July 2017
Patient protests planned discharge due to unresolved foot pain and unanswered symptoms.
11 July 2017
Discharged from hospital with diagnosis of idiopathic Parkinson’s disease.
13 July 2017
DAT scan performed and interpreted as consistent with Parkinsonian disease.
September–October 2017
Anticholinergic medication produces dramatic improvement in symptoms including foot pain.
2018–2022
Multiple Parkinson’s medications introduced and adjusted while core symptoms persist.
2025
Formal challenges raised regarding diagnostic sequence and medical record accuracy.
February 2026
Genetic testing confirms pathogenic PRKN variants establishing a monogenic cause of Parkinsonism and dystonia.
