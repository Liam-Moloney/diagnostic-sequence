Early History

2004
First persistent foot pain appears during bar work. Symptoms initially treated as local foot problems such as corns and calluses.

2009–2010
Repeated podiatry treatment for foot problems. Underlying cause remains unexplained.

2013
Symptoms misattributed to anxiety by a GP. Psychiatric assessment shortly afterwards finds no psychiatric disorder.

Referral and Hospital Admission

April 2017
New GP begins referral process due to worsening gait disturbance and chronic foot pain.

29 June 2017
Urgent GP referral to neurology describing abnormal gait and possible Parkinsonian features.

3 July 2017
Admission to University Hospital Galway for neurological assessment.

4 July 2017
Case meeting held. Dopaminergic treatment is discussed but not initiated.

5–9 July 2017
Hospital investigations including imaging and neurological assessments.

9 July 2017
Long Term Illness certification completed for Parkinson’s disease.

10 July 2017
Patient protests planned discharge, raising unresolved foot pain as the central problem.

11 July 2017
Discharged with diagnosis of idiopathic Parkinson’s disease.

13 July 2017
DAT scan performed and interpreted as consistent with Parkinsonian disease.

Post-Discharge Developments

September–October 2017
Anticholinergic medication produces dramatic improvement in symptoms including foot pain.

2018
Follow-up neurology review maintains Parkinson’s diagnosis despite unusual medication response.

2018–2022
Multiple Parkinson’s medications introduced and adjusted. Core foot-centred symptoms persist.

Later Investigation

2025
Formal complaints and record challenges initiated concerning diagnostic sequence and record accuracy.

February 2026
Genetic testing confirms two pathogenic PRKN gene variants establishing a monogenic cause of Parkinsonism and dystonia.

Present

The project archive documents the sequence of events leading from the 2017 hospital admission to the eventual genetic confirmation of the underlying condition.
